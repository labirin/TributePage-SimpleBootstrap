Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie (Indonesian pronunciation: [baxaruddin jusuf habibi] (About this sound listen); born 25 June 1936) is an Indonesian engineer who was President of Indonesia from 1998 to 1999. He succeeded Suharto, who resigned in 1998. His presidency is seen as a transition to the post-Suharto era. Upon becoming president, he liberalized Indonesia's press and political party laws, and held an early democratic election in 1999, which resulted in the end of his presidency. His presidency was the third, and the shortest, after independence.

Here's a time line of B. J. Habibie's life:

  • 1936 – Born in Parepare, South Sulawesi, Indonesia
  • 1960 – Received an engineer’s degree in aeronautical engineering with the title Diplom-Ingenieur from the RWTH (Rhein Westfalen Aachen Technische Hochschule) Aachen University in Aachen, Germany with cum laude predicate
  • 196 – Married to Hasri Ainun Besari
  • 1965 – Received a Doktor-ingenieur from the Aachen University with summa cum laude predicate. At the same year after completed his doctorate, he started to work at Messerchmitt-Bölkow-Bolhm (an aviation company centered in Hamburg, Germany) as a head of research and development of aircraft structure until 1969. During his period of working in this company, he Invented Habibie factor, Habibie Theorem and Habibie method used in thermodynamics, construction and aerodynamics disciplines respectively
  • 1969 – Appointed as a head of division of methods and technology in industrial commercial and military aircraft at Messerchmitt-Bölkow-Bolhm until 1973
  • 1974 – Appointed as a vice president in technology at Messerchmitt-Bölkow-Bolhm
  • 1974 – Return to his homeland in Indonesia and recruited by the second President of Indonesia, Suharto, to drive industrialization and development of Indonesia
  • 1976 – Became the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of Industri Pesawat Terbang Nusantara (IPTN) which is the first Indonesian state-owned aviation enterprise in Indonesia. Later it became Indonesian Aerospace (Dirgantara)
  • 1977 – Received an engineering professor title from Bandung Institute of Technology, Indonesia
  • 1978 – Appointed as Indonesian Minister of Research and Technology until 1998
  • 1990 – Appointed a Fellow of the Royal Academy of Engineering (FReng), England
  • 1993 – Awarded an Honorary Fellow of the Royal Aeronautical Society (HonFRAes), England
  • 1995 – Lead the development of the first Indonesian airplane N250 Gatot Kaca
  • 1998 – Became a Vice President of Republic of Indonesia
  • 1998 – Became a third President of Republic of Indonesia succeeded the resigned former president, Suharto, until 1999. This is the most difficult period for him as a president due to chaotic social, political and economic situation post resignation of Suharto. However, he successfully led his country to stabilize progressively out from the crisis by implementing the reform policies and regulation particularly in securing economy, freedom of speech, press and media, foundation for anti-monopoly and fair competition, acknowledgment of human right and many other reformations. New president election was held in 1999 and he was not among the president candidate
  • 1998 – Gave a referendum for East Timor, offering choice between special autonomy and independence
  • 2006 – Released a book Detik-Detik Yang Menentukan: Jalan Panjang Indonesia Menuju Demokrasi (Decisive Moments: Indonesia’s Long Road Towards Democracy)
  • 2010 – His beloved wife passed away
  • 2010 – Awarded Honorary PhD degree in Technology by the University of Indonesia
  • Nowadays – Active in the organization he established namely Habibie Center focusing on the advancement and modernization of democracy in Indonesia which fundamentally based on morality, cultural integrity and religious values
